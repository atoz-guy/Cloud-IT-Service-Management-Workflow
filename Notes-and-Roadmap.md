**Candidate Professional Development Roadmap: PHDA Certification**

**Objective:** Successfully achieve the Practical Help Desk Associate (PHDA) certification to demonstrate hands-on proficiency in IT support, ticketing systems, and active environment troubleshooting.

**Core Competency Focus Areas:**
To ensure readiness, I am tracking my proficiency across the four pillars of the Practical Help Desk curriculum:
•	Operating System Utilities: Mastery of Windows/Linux command-line tools for diagnostics and system health monitoring.
•	Active Directory (IAM): Practical experience in account management, group policy enforcement, and permission troubleshooting.
•	Networking Basics: Proficient use of diagnostic protocols (ICMP, DHCP, DNS) to isolate and resolve connectivity issues.
•	Remote Support & Ticketing: Demonstrated ability to manage the full lifecycle of an IT ticket—from initial discovery to resolution and formal Knowledge Base documentation.

**Documentation Methodology:**
I am building a verifiable portfolio of my progress in my Cloud-IT-Service-Management-Workflow repository. Each entry follows a rigorous, standardized format:
1.	Initial Assessment: Context and impact analysis.
2.	Troubleshooting Steps: Logical, tool-based diagnostic progression.
3.	Resolution: Execution of the corrective action.
4.	Verification: Confirmation of successful restoration of service.
5.	Root Cause Analysis: Preventing future recurrences.
Strategic Goal: By systematically completing these simulated case studies, I am gaining the "day-in-the-life" experience required to excel in an entry-level help desk position. This process is building the technical muscle memory and the documentation discipline essential for passing the hands-on PHDA exam.
